Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931-2015) was an Indian scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Renowned as the "Missile Man" for his role in India's missile development program, Kalam's innovative work in aerospace technology earned him widespread acclaim. His presidency was marked by his emphasis on youth empowerment and national development. Known for his humility and inspirational leadership, Kalam authored several books and remained a beloved figure in Indian public life, advocating for education and scientific advancement.
